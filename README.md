## The Non-Repaint Claim: What It Actually Means

The product page states "No Repaint" as a feature. This is worth understanding before you put any tool on a live chart.

A repainting indicator changes its past signals as new price data comes in — so when you look back at the chart history, all the arrows appear to be in perfect positions, even though they moved *after* the fact. It creates a misleading picture of accuracy.

A non-repainting indicator locks each signal in place the moment it generates. What you see is what you would have actually seen in real time. That's the honest standard for evaluating any signal tool, and Scorpio Scalper's page explicitly claims to meet it.

That said — independently verifying this on a demo account before committing to live trades is always worth doing. Attach the indicator, watch a few sessions, and confirm that historical arrows aren't shifting positions as new candles form.
